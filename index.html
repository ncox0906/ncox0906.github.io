Increasing Profits of a Food Distribution Company


A business was having supply issues with one of its product lines. This lack of supply was causing profits to decrease. I looked into other parts of the business and used RFM and K-means clustering to identify areas where more profits could be generated. I presented a list of products and stores to target to the business. I also created multiple dashboards (links located below) to allow the business owners to explore more of the data on their own. I found my recommendations would generate a 2.2% increase in profits.


Dashboards: Map of Stores and their Gross Profits, Stores to Target, Profits and Product Sales

Helping Dogs in Shelters that Experience Long Adoption Wait Times


In this group project, my group and I tried to help shelter dogs get a new home. We started with datasets, provided by Austin Animal Center and The Intelligence of Dogs dataset (1994). Our idea was to identify dogs AT RISK to experience extremely long adoption wait times, and help these dogs to be adopted faster. To be able to make predictions we had to clean our data and use a variety of features to find valuable correlations.

While building a model we realized that we can make an App, which can help dogs at risk all over the country to be adopted. So we used StreamLit to create an App that is able to gather up to date data from hundreds of shelters by web scraping Petfinder.com and filter dogs at higher risk.

Use the App we made to find a dog near you! Explore the shelter dogs in our data using this Tableau Dashboard.


Data Scientists that contributed to this project: Gene Woodstock, Anna Rutledge, and Elena Yakubchik

Predicting What Subreddit a Comment Comes From


This project uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) to determine what subreddit a comment comes from. I used the Pushshift Reddit API to gather more than 115,000 comments from two very similar subreddits. After thoroughly cleaning the data, I used a voting ensemble between a Multinomial Naive Bayes and Logistic Regression model. This model ended up beating the baseline accuracy by 17.6 percentage points for predicting what subreddit a comment came from.
